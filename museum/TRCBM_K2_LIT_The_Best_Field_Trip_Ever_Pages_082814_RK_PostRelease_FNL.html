The Best

Field Trip Ever

by Mary Hertz Scarbrough

Illustrations by Sanjay Charlton

Ms. Wu’s class was planning a field trip for Tuesday. They had voted on what they’d like to see.

“Three ideas got the most votes,” Ms. Wu said. “Most of you are interested in dinosaurs, robots, sharks, and sports.”

“Is there a way to see them all?” Lucas asked.

“Well,” Ms. Wu said, “the Science Center has dinosaurs, robots, and sharks.”

“No sports?” Mark asked.

“Sorry, no sports,” said Ms. Wu.

On Tuesday, the class took the bus to the Science Center.

Outside the center, they spotted a dinosaur model.

“Cool!” said Mark. “Let’s see the dinosaurs first!” The other kids nodded.

They headed to the Hall of Dinosaurs. They saw huge dinosaur bones and also got to open and close a dinosaur’s jaw.

Next, Ms. Wu took the class to another room.

She said, “This is the dinosaur dig room. You can dig for fossils here!”

Soon the kids were brushing away sand from bones.

Katy pointed to a bone. “It looks like a tooth!”

Then the class went to the shark pool.
They looked through the glass. They watched divers feed the sharks chunks of fish.

“Sharks have big teeth!” said Dani.

“Not as big as a dinosaur tooth,”
said Lucas.

They went to the Robot Room next. The class tried to beat a robot at a game. But the robot was too fast!

Then they made robot cars and raced them on a track.

“Let’s visit the Good Health Room,”
Ms. Wu said.

“That doesn’t sound very fun,” said Mark.

“I have a feeling you’ll like this!” Ms. Wu said.

In the Good Health Room, a woman showed the class how to do karate.

“Wow,” said Mark. “We got to see a sport after all!”

The whole class practiced karate moves.

“This was the best field trip ever,” they said.

“The best is that we got to see dinosaurs, robots, and sharks!” said Katy.

“And don’t forget sports!” said Mark.
